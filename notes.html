Eklavya: Notes

I used to note things down in notebooks made of paper, but they are hard to edit, hard to share, and quite non-portable. So now I use digital formats, like PDF and HTML.

No single format is appropriate for all of my notes, so they are stored in different places, and each place has a different format. Here are links to most of them.

  1. TheoremDep

    Github: sharmaeklavya2/theoremdep-source.
    • TheoremDep is a website that lists many theorems and shows the dependencies between them. Theorem X is said to be dependent on theorem Y iff Y is used as a lemma in the proof of X.
    • I created TheoremDep to better organize and document my own knowledge. TheoremDep mainly contains theorems on abstract algebra, linear algebra, number theory, graph theory, bin-packing and probability.
    • Theorems are authored in JSON+Markdown+MathJax. ConcepDAG converts them to an HTML+MathJax website.

  2. Standalone Notes

    Github: sharmaeklavya2/notes-source.
    • Some of my notes don't fit into the ‘graph of theorems’ representation, so instead of adding them to TheoremDep, I use a more self-contained format for them.
    • The notes are authored in LaTeX and markdown. LaTeX is compiled to PDF and markdown is compiled to HTML.

  3. Article Gists

    Github: sharmaeklavya2/articleGists.
    • List of results in research papers I read. Useful for surveying a topic if it contains enough content there.

  4. Web of Reductions

    Github: sharmaeklavya2/web-of-reductions.
    • A graphical representation of reductions among NP-complete poblems.
    • I extended this into a general tool for visualizing graphs (source code), but I haven't yet used that for anything.
